Section one

What Aximo is.

So, what is Aximo, in one sentence?

A small Kenya-focused advisory platform that runs a paid Business Diagnostic, then matches you to a vetted expert partner from one of five disciplines — Legal, Finance, Marketing, Market Entry, or Technology — and holds quality on the work until handover.

Is this a consultancy?

Not in the traditional sense. A consultancy sells its own time by the hour. We sell the discipline of choosing the right specialist and scoping the work properly before money moves. The specialist is paid by you, in full, for what you scope together. We are paid for the Diagnostic and for a transparent introducer fee on engagements that go ahead.

Who is this for?

Foreign companies entering Kenya for the first time. Diaspora founders returning to build. Kenyan SMEs in the KSh 1M–50M range that are ready to scale. Emerging tech teams that need to move through regulatory complexity without losing six months. If you do not see yourself in those four, the Diagnostic is probably the wrong starting point and we will say so on the intake call.

"We don't sell information, we deliver access." What does that actually mean?

Most market entry advice is already on the internet. The bottleneck is not information; it is the warm introduction to the right advocate, the banker who returns your call, the regulator who knows your name. Our job is to compress the months it takes to build those relationships into a single brief and a matched engagement.

Section two

The Diagnostic.

What do I actually receive?

A 60-minute working session with an Aximo principal, then a written brief delivered inside 48 hours. The brief maps your situation in three layers: the gaps that matter most, the route forward in priority order, and the shape of the engagement we would recommend — which practice, what scope, which kind of partner. You keep the brief whether or not you choose to engage further.

Why do I pay for a discovery call?

Free discovery filters for the wrong things. It rewards salesmanship over rigour and attracts tyre-kickers. Charging for the Diagnostic does three things: it filters for operators who are serious about acting, it pays for the senior time that goes into a useful brief, and it removes our incentive to oversell whatever comes next. The Diagnostic stands on its own.

What does it cost?

A single transparent figure, shared on the intake form once we confirm Aximo is a fit. No surprise fees, no retainer hidden in a follow-up email. If the Diagnostic leads to a scoped engagement, the fee is credited toward it.

How quickly can we start?

Most Diagnostics are booked inside seven working days of intake. The session can be in person in Nairobi or on a video call. The written brief follows within 48 hours. If the work that comes after is urgent, we can usually match you to a partner inside a further week.

And if the brief is not useful to me?

Write to us inside 14 days and we will refund the fee in full. One line on what we missed, no further argument. We have not had to issue one yet — the offer is there in writing.

Section three

The network.

How do you vet your partners?

Three checks, in order. Credentials and regulator standing, verified at the source rather than from a CV. A reference call with two recent clients, conducted by an Aximo principal. A trial scope — a small piece of work under our observation, paid by us. We carry no partner we have not seen ship clean work for someone else first.

How large is the network?

Intentionally small. We would rather have three excellent advocates in Legal than thirty average ones. The network is sized to the volume of work the Diagnostic produces, not to a marketing claim. We turn away more applications than we accept, and we publish no partner directory because the value is the matching, not the list.

What if the match is not working out?

You tell us. We stay close to every engagement until handover, with a structured check-in at the midpoint. If something is off, we are the first call, not the last resort. We will either fix the relationship, re-match you to a different partner in the same practice, or refund the introducer fee. Partners do not get to mark their own homework.

Do you take cuts from your partners?

No. The introducer fee comes from you, up front, disclosed before anything is signed. We never take a hidden cut from the partner's side because that quietly incentivises overselling the work. Your partner is paid by you, in full, for what you scoped together.

Section four

Money & logistics.

Shillings or dollars?

Either. Kenyan operators are billed in KSh against a published rate. Foreign and diaspora clients can choose USD, billed against the same underlying figure at the day's rate. Invoices are issued by a Kenyan-registered entity with a tax PIN, so the expense is clean on either side.

Can the whole thing be remote?

Most of it. The Diagnostic, the brief, status calls and reviews all work over video. Where physical presence matters — a regulator visit, a banking introduction, a site walk — your partner will be in the room and will report back the same day. We do not pretend a video call is a substitute for a handshake when it is not.

Will you sign an NDA before we talk?

Yes — on your paper or ours, before the intake call. Our standard NDA is two pages, mutual, and we will sign yours on a quick lawyer review if it is reasonable. Confidentiality is table stakes; you should not have to ask twice.

What if Aximo is the wrong fit for my problem?

We will say so on the intake call, before any money changes hands. If we know someone better placed — inside Kenya or outside it — we will introduce you. Bad matches are the fastest way to lose the trust of an ecosystem this size. We have a strong commercial interest in not taking work we cannot do well.

Section five

Getting started.

What does the first step look like in practice?

You fill in a short intake form. We read it the same day. If we are a fit, we send a calendar link with the Diagnostic fee and a one-page agreement. You book the session, the session happens, the brief follows. The whole arc from "wrote to Aximo" to "have a brief in hand" usually lands inside two weeks.

Can I bring my team into the Diagnostic session?

Yes — up to three people from your side. We would rather have your CFO or country lead in the room than re-explain the brief later. We will tell you in advance who the right voices are for the session based on the intake form.

Is there anything you will not work on?

We do not take work in gambling, payday lending, or sectors that operate outside Kenyan regulation. We also do not take engagements where the operator wants a stamp of approval more than a candid assessment. We can usually tell on the intake call, and we say so plainly.

Where do I write to you?

hello@aximohub.com — read by a person, not a queue. For anything time-sensitive or with attachments, the intake form is the cleanest path in.
